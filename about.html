Introduction

Dr. Suma Prasad Kantipudi
Director

Dr. Suma Prasad Kantipudi, Director of Prasad Hospitals India Pvt. Ltd. and Prasad Research Foundation, is a distinguished figure in the realm of Infertility and Stem cell research, honoured with a patent on developing novel products in Stem cell therapies. Dr. Suma is known among Physician circles to solve difficult problems in medicine and novel approaches evaluating impact and iterative refinement.

Born as a second girl child, my parents never gave up on the quality of life and education despite their petty jobs in an expensive city like Hyderabad. Tutored by my dad to be the first doctor in the family. I can never forget those days when my mother worked in a small stores and carry groceries bag home.

I can’t say its hard work by my parents as I always noticed enormous love and giving whatever they have to both their daughters till date, may be it is pure passion and love to their kids.

Life began as a 2nd year Medico at Gandhi Medical College and evenings to late nights as an assistant to Dr. K. V. R. Prasad at Sridevi Nursing Home, Secunderabad during early 80’s.

I learnt over the decades that to solve a difficult problem in medicine, may it be an infertile couple struggling to make a baby or a non-healing diabetic wound in a 70-year-old – success came only when I remained persuasive.

When the infertile couple succeeded and while delivering that baby, the first cry was music to my ears. Ever since my journey through treating those desperate infertile couples and training my juniors with to handle patients with care and empathy made life meaningful to myself and many more.

“In nothing do men more nearly approach God than giving health to man” — Cicero (106 BC – 46 BC)

Novelty in life came with clinical research in stem cell therapies while I continued to run my medical practice where I was mentored to preserve each precious cell from the leftover’s after baby’s birth – the umbilical cord and placenta.

I remember the feeling of joy almost to tears the day I discharged my first patient from the hospital who was posted for amputation of leg due to chronic non-healing diabetic foot ulcer.

It took hours convincing my fellow diabetologist and the surgeon to use the patented product showcasing the regulatory approvals and proof-of-concepts. A week later, the patient walked out of the hospital on both his feet after thanking me enough and touching my feet.

What I learnt is nothing more than curiosity about nature’s mechanism and its healing powers and the rest will follow – “Do basic research”.

The honour of being trusted to give advice and the gratitude of helping someone through a difficult illness – these things never go old.

As a doctor, I tell my juniors to never forget that we have the opportunities to do more good in one day than most of the people have in a year. And we get this opportunity every day, even on a Sunday.

While the journey as a doctor, as a daughter and as a mother seems long and hard but perseverance and love to embrace all situations is the only way I learnt to sail through.

Well the life story continues, as I never give-up the zeal to practice medicine and to be a researcher building teams and teaching them to dedicate their energy to develop novel methods and develop newer therapies in this ever enigmatous Stem cell research.

Every milligram of tissue of the leftover umbilical cord can be translated to a ‘Sanjeevani’. I would still continue my medical practice despite all the difficulties and road blocks just to practice medicine, truly its worth it.
